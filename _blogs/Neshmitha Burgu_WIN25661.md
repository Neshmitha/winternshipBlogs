title: "From Passive Watching to Active Thinking: What This Internship Changed for Me"

author: Neshmitha Burgu

nptel\_id: WIN25661



When I started this internship, I thought learning meant watching videos, completing tasks, and moving on to the next item on the list. I believed that as long as I was “doing something,” I was learning. This internship quietly challenged that assumption.



In the beginning, I approached the sessions the way I had approached many online courses before — listen, understand a little, and hope things would make sense later. But as the days passed, I realized that this journey was not designed for passive participation. It demanded presence, attention, and thinking.



Working through the ViBe platform made this clear very early. Learning was no longer about letting a video play in the background. It required focus, reflection, and immediate engagement through quizzes. Initially, this felt uncomfortable. But over time, I understood something important: "**real learning begins when distractions end"**.



Case studies added another layer to this experience. They were not about getting the “right answer” quickly. They forced me to sit with confusion, discuss ideas with others, and reason through problems. At times, I felt stuck or unsure, but those moments taught me more than easy solutions ever could. I started valuing the process of thinking rather than just reaching an outcome.



The discussions in breakout rooms also changed how I learn. Hearing different perspectives showed me that there is never just one way to think about a problem. Learning became collaborative rather than individual. I wasn’t just absorbing information anymore — I was actively engaging with it.



One of the biggest changes I noticed was in my mindset. I stopped chasing completion and started chasing clarity. Instead of asking, “How fast can I finish this?”, I began asking, “Do I really understand this?” That shift, made a huge difference.



This internship didn’t dramatically change my skills overnight. What it changed was far more important — how I approach learning itself. I now understand that growth doesn’t come from passive watching, but from active thinking, consistency, and honest effort.



As I move forward, I know I’ll carry this lesson with me. Not just into future internships or projects, but into every learning experience that follows.
